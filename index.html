Introduction

Table Tennis is a competitive sport that is played globally. This sport is also known as Ping Pong. This sport needs a minimum of two players and a maximum of 4 players. The objective of the sport of to hit a light weighted ball across the net using a small racket. This is a zero contact sport, but it is mentally challenging.

Brief History

Table Tennis is traced back to Victorian England. It was a form of amusement for the upper class in the 1880s. They used everyday objects. For example, books were used as a net, a knot of string as a ball, and a cigar box lid as a racket. The popularity grew and company started to sell the equipment commercially.

The "sound" effect while playing the game was "Ping-Pong". This was the given name at the time. Around the 20th century, plastic balls and rackets were introduced to the game. Since it was so popular by 1927, London England, held the first world Championships. Since it was a big success, Table Tennis was granted an Olympic sport status in 1988. As in current, the sport is dominated by Asian country, such as, China and Japan.

After changing the size of the ball, in 2001, the ITTF (International Table Tennis Federation) change the traditional 21 point scoring system to a 11 point system. Even though the scoring the system, people still use the 21 point game is still used widely at recreational level.
